### **Practical Accessibility - Sample Sentences for Questions**

---

#### **Q3: Do you prefer listening to recorded music or attending live performances? Why?**
1. "Recorded music is **easily accessible**, allowing me to listen to a variety of genres and artists without needing to attend specific events."
2. "Attending live performances ensures a unique experience that is not **universally accessible** through recorded music."
3. "Listening to recorded music offers **on-demand access**, enabling me to enjoy it anytime, unlike the scheduled nature of live events."

---

#### **Q16: Do you agree that newspapers are a better source of current news than television? Why or why not?**
1. "Television is more **widely accessible**, delivering breaking news instantly to a broader audience compared to newspapers."
2. "Newspapers often provide **greater depth and analysis**, which may not always be available in quick television updates."
3. "While newspapers can improve **accessibility to in-depth reporting**, television excels in live coverage and immediate news dissemination."

---

#### **Q72: Should schools limit students to one extracurricular activity to ensure enough time for studying? Why or why not?**
1. "Limiting extracurricular activities can ensure **greater access** to academic resources and reduce distractions from studies."
2. "Participating in multiple activities provides **equal access** to various skill-building opportunities that one activity alone might not offer."
3. "Restricting extracurriculars may **enhance accessibility** to focused learning time, but it could also limit personal growth and exploration."

---

#### **Q71: Should universities close computer labs since students now own laptops? Why or why not?**
1. "Keeping computer labs open ensures **equal access** to advanced technology for students who may not own high-performance laptops."
2. "Closing computer labs could hinder students who need **on-demand access** to specialized software or network resources."
3. "Maintaining labs improves **universally accessible** computing environments, especially for group projects and collaborative work."

---

#### **Q63: Should all first-year students take a class on using university library resources and writing research papers? Why?**
1. "Requiring this class would **enhance accessibility** to valuable research tools, ensuring all students can navigate the library effectively."
2. "Teaching library skills early allows students to gain **greater access** to academic content that can benefit them throughout their studies."
3. "This course would promote **equal access** to resources, equipping every student with essential skills for academic success."

### **Collocations in Context**

- **easily accessible**: "Listening to recorded music is easily accessible, providing convenience and variety at my fingertips."
- **widely accessible**: "Television news is widely accessible, ensuring that even those in remote areas stay informed."
- **enhance accessibility**: "Mandatory library courses enhance accessibility to research tools for first-year students."
- **on-demand access**: "Recorded music provides on-demand access, making it perfect for listening during busy schedules."
- **equal access**: "Universities should maintain computer labs to ensure equal access to technology for all students."
- **universally accessible**: "Efforts to make educational resources universally accessible benefit students of all backgrounds."
