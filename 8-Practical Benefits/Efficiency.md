#### **Q6: Some teachers prefer students to send questions about coursework by email. Other teachers prefer in-person discussions. Which do you think is better? Explain why.**
1. "Emailing questions ensures **time-efficient** communication, allowing both students and teachers to address concerns at their convenience."  
2. "In-person discussions can **boost efficiency** by providing immediate clarification and reducing the need for follow-up questions."  
3. "Using email for queries helps **streamline processes**, as responses are documented for future reference."

1. "Working with technology is **highly efficient**, as it enables the automation of repetitive tasks and reduces manual effort."  
2. "Jobs involving minimal technology may improve focus but lack the ability to **streamline processes** as effectively as tech-based roles."  
3. "Technology-driven roles are designed to **boost efficiency**, making them ideal for individuals who prioritize productivity."

3. "This practice can enhance **workflow optimization**, as it allows employees to recharge and tackle tasks with renewed energy."

2. "University computer labs provide **time-efficient** solutions for students who may experience technical difficulties with personal devices."  
3. "Closing computer labs could compromise **overall efficiency**, especially for those relying on specialized resources available only on campus."

#### **Q64: When giving a lecture to a small class, some professors prefer to wait until the end of class to answer student questions. Other professors will stop at different points during the lecture to answer questions. Which style do you think is best for learning? Explain why.**
1. "Answering questions during the lecture promotes **workflow optimization**, as it addresses doubts in real time and keeps students engaged."  
2. "Waiting until the end ensures **time-efficient** delivery of the lecture, allowing the professor to complete their material without interruption."  
3. "Answering questions throughout can **streamline processes** by addressing concepts immediately, ensuring better understanding."
