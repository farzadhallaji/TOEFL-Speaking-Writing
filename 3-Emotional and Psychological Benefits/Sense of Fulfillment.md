### **Q4: Do you agree or disagree with the statement that the most important lessons can’t be learned in a classroom?**
1. "Important life lessons often come from real-world experiences, which provide a **fulfilling experience** by teaching resilience and adaptability."
2. "Classrooms impart knowledge, but facing challenges outside academic settings fosters **emotional growth** and practical problem-solving skills."
3. "Learning through hands-on experiences offers a **sense of achievement**, as individuals gain confidence by overcoming obstacles."

### **Q36: Some recent university graduates choose to spend a period of time doing volunteer or community service before they start their careers. Others think it is better to start working as soon as possible after graduation. Which view do you agree with?**
1. "Volunteering provides a **sense of purpose**, allowing graduates to contribute meaningfully to society before entering the workforce."
2. "Engaging in community service creates a **deeply rewarding** experience by helping others and fostering personal growth."
3. "While starting work immediately may seem practical, volunteering offers a chance to make **meaningful contributions**, which enrich one’s perspective and career readiness."

2. "Working during university fosters **personal satisfaction**, as it helps students develop independence and financial management skills."
3. "Engaging in part-time work offers a **fulfilling experience**, as students gain real-world skills that complement their academic learning."

3. "An evolving personality brings a **sense of purpose**, as changes reflect growth and resilience in response to life’s challenges."
