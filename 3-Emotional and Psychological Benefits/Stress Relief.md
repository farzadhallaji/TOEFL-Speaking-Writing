### **Q6: Some teachers prefer students to send questions about coursework and assignments by email. Other teachers prefer students ask these questions in person. Which do you think is better? Explain why.**
1. "Asking questions in person provides immediate clarity, which can **reduce mental tension** and prevent prolonged stress over assignments."
2. "Sending emails offers a **stress-free option** for students who feel anxious about face-to-face interactions, allowing them to process their queries thoughtfully."
3. "Both approaches can **relieve stress**, depending on whether a student values instant feedback or time to formulate questions."

### **Q19: Some college students take classes during the summer. Others take the summer off from school, so that they can rest or work at a job. Which do you think is better for students? Explain why.**
1. "Taking the summer off allows students to engage in **stress-relieving activities**, such as traveling or pursuing hobbies, which rejuvenates them for the next semester."
2. "Working during summer can **release anxiety** about financial burdens, helping students feel more prepared for the upcoming academic year."
3. "Attending summer classes might promote continuity, but a balanced break often leads to greater **mental relaxation** and improved focus later."

### **Q32: Imagine that you live in a crowded city with only one green space—the city park. Do you think building housing on the park is a good idea? Why or why not?**
1. "Green spaces provide a **natural stress reliever**, offering city residents a peaceful retreat from the bustling urban environment."
2. "Removing the park would eliminate opportunities to **promote relaxation**, which are essential for mental and physical well-being in a crowded city."
3. "Keeping the park intact ensures a **therapeutic experience** for everyone, as access to nature is vital for stress relief and overall health."

### **Q70: Should parents read children’s messages without permission?**
1. "Respecting children’s privacy helps create a **stress-free home environment**, reducing conflicts and fostering trust within the family."
2. "Parents who find a **privacy balance** between oversight and independence can ease family tensions while addressing safety concerns."
3. "When parents communicate openly rather than snooping, it can **relieve stress** for both parties, ensuring mutual respect."
