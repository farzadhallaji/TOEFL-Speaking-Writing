### Confidence-building

### **Q14: Do you prefer jobs that require you to work with technology or without it? Why?**
1. "Jobs involving technology **build confidence** by equipping individuals with skills that are in high demand in today’s workforce."
2. "Non-technical roles can **foster self-belief** through creativity and interpersonal interactions, boosting confidence in communication."
3. "Both roles **develop poise**, as they challenge individuals to adapt and excel in their respective domains."

### **Q77: Should parents discourage children from pursuing competitive careers? Why?**
1. "Competitive careers **pursue aspirations** that drive personal growth, even if they come with challenges."
2. "Such careers **build resilience**, teaching children to navigate setbacks and believe in their abilities."
3. "Encouraging children to explore competitive fields helps them **foster self-belief**, essential for long-term success."

### **Q110: Is intelligence the most important factor for academic success? Why?**
1. "While intelligence plays a role, **self-confidence** and persistence are often more critical for achieving goals."
2. "Holistic success depends on factors like emotional intelligence, which **fosters self-belief** and enhances collaboration."
3. "The ability to **develop poise** under academic pressure often outweighs innate intelligence in determining success."

### **Q91: Professors eligible for promotions/pay raises based on student evaluations?**
1. "Positive student evaluations can **build confidence** in professors, affirming their teaching methods and effectiveness."
2. "Feedback from students helps professors **pursue aspirations** of continuous improvement and professional growth."
3. "Recognition through evaluations fosters **self-assurance**, motivating professors to innovate and excel."
