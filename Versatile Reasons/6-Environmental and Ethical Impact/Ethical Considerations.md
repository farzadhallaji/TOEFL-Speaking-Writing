### Ethical Considerations


### **Q21: Do you prefer buying new or used books? Why?**
1. "Buying used books supports **ethical consumerism** by reducing waste and promoting sustainability."
2. "Choosing used books demonstrates **responsible practices**, ensuring that resources are reused effectively."
3. "New books, on the other hand, contribute to **cultural investment** by supporting authors and publishers."

### **Q22: Are celebrities good role models for young people? Why or why not?**
1. "Celebrities who engage in **ethical responsibility** and philanthropy set positive examples for young people."
2. "However, some public figures fail to **promote fairness**, highlighting the importance of discerning role models."
3. "Young people can learn to balance admiration with critical thinking, emphasizing **responsible practices** in their own lives."

### **Q34: Should businesses be required to spend profits on social programs? Why?**
1. "Requiring businesses to fund social programs reinforces their **ethical responsibility** to contribute to societal well-being."
2. "Investing in social initiatives fosters **responsible practices**, enhancing a company’s reputation and community impact."
3. "These efforts promote **fairness** by addressing inequities and supporting underprivileged communities."

### **Q48: Should governments support arts institutions? Why or why not?**
1. "Funding arts institutions reflects **cultural investment**, preserving heritage and fostering creativity."
2. "Governments prioritizing the arts demonstrate **ethical responsibility**, ensuring equal access to cultural enrichment."
3. "Public support for the arts **promotes fairness**, allowing artists from all backgrounds to thrive."

### **Q62: Building a housing complex on the last green space in a city?**
