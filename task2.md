## Templates

### **Template 1: Disagreement with a Proposal or Policy**

1. **Introduction:**
   - Briefly summarize the proposal or policy.
   - State the speaker’s disagreement.
   - Example: *The proposal suggests [briefly summarize plan/policy]. The speaker disagrees with this and provides two main reasons.*
2. **Reason 1:**
   - Present the first reason for disagreement with details.
   - Example: *First, the speaker argues that [reason]. For example, they mention that [supporting detail].*
3. **Reason 2:**
   - Present the second reason for disagreement with elaboration.
   - Example: *Second, they believe that [reason]. This is supported by their point that [specific example].*
4. **Conclusion:**
   - Summarize the disagreement and reasons.
   - Example: *In conclusion, the speaker opposes the proposal due to [reason 1] and [reason 2].*

------

### **Template 2: Agreement with a Proposal or Policy**

1. **Introduction:**
   - Briefly summarize the proposal or policy.
   - State the speaker’s agreement.
   - Example: *The proposal suggests [briefly summarize plan/policy]. The speaker agrees with this idea and provides two reasons.*
2. **Reason 1:**
   - Explain the first reason for agreement with supporting details.
   - Example: *First, the speaker supports this idea because [reason]. They mention that [supporting detail].*
3. **Reason 2:**
   - Explain the second reason for agreement with elaboration.
   - Example: *Second, they feel that [reason]. For instance, they explain that [specific example].*
4. **Conclusion:**
   - Summarize the agreement and reasons.
   - Example: *To conclude, the speaker approves of the proposal because [reason 1] and [reason 2].*

------

### **Template 3: Mixed Reaction or Balanced Opinion**

1. **Introduction:**
   - Briefly summarize the proposal or announcement.
   - Mention the speaker’s mixed opinion.
   - Example: *The proposal suggests [briefly summarize plan]. The speaker has mixed feelings about it, supporting some aspects but opposing others.*
2. **Support:**
   - Explain what the speaker agrees with and why.
   - Example: *On the one hand, the speaker supports [aspect]. They think this is a good idea because [reason/supporting detail].*
3. **Opposition:**
   - Describe what the speaker disagrees with and why.
   - Example: *On the other hand, they disagree with [aspect]. They argue that [reason/supporting detail].*
4. **Conclusion:**
   - Summarize the mixed opinion.
   - Example: *In conclusion, the speaker finds some merit in the plan but opposes other parts due to [reason].*

------

### **Template 4: Critique of Implementation**

1. **Introduction:**
   - Summarize the proposal and the speaker’s critique.
   - Example: *The announcement proposes [briefly summarize plan/policy]. The speaker critiques the implementation of this plan for two reasons.*
2. **Reason 1:**
   - Explain the first problem with implementation.
   - Example: *First, the speaker believes that [reason]. They highlight that [supporting detail].*
3. **Reason 2:**
   - Explain the second issue with implementation.
   - Example: *Second, they argue that [reason]. For instance, they mention [specific example].*
4. **Conclusion:**
   - Summarize the critique.
   - Example: *Overall, the speaker feels the plan’s implementation is flawed due to [reason 1] and [reason 2].*

------

### **Template 5: Explanation of an Opinion**

1. **Introduction:**
   - Briefly state the announcement and the speaker’s opinion.
   - Example: *The announcement is about [briefly summarize]. The speaker believes that [opinion].*
2. **Reason 1:**
   - Present the first reason for the opinion.
   - Example: *First, the speaker argues that [reason]. For instance, they mention [supporting detail].*
3. **Reason 2:**
   - Present the second reason for the opinion.
   - Example: *Second, they feel that [reason]. This is supported by their point that [specific example].*
4. **Conclusion:**
   - Summarize the opinion and reasons.
   - Example: *To conclude, the speaker believes [opinion] because of [reason 1] and [reason 2].*

------

### **Template 6: Comparison Between Two Plans**

1. **Introduction:**
   - Mention the two plans being compared and the speaker’s preference.
   - Example: *The announcement discusses two plans: [briefly summarize both]. The speaker prefers [plan] for two main reasons.*
2. **Reason 1:**
   - Explain why the speaker prefers one plan over the other.
   - Example: *First, they argue that [reason]. They explain that [supporting detail].*
3. **Reason 2:**
   - Further elaborate on the preference.
   - Example: *Second, they feel that [reason]. For instance, they highlight [specific example].*
4. **Conclusion:**
   - Summarize the comparison and preference.
   - Example: *In conclusion, the speaker prefers [plan] because [reason 1] and [reason 2].*

------

### **Template 7: Summarizing and Explaining Two Opinions**

1. **Introduction:**
   - Mention the topic and summarize both opinions.
   - Example: *The conversation discusses [topic]. One speaker supports [opinion 1], while the other disagrees and provides reasons.*
2. **Supporter’s Opinion:**
   - Briefly explain the reasons for agreement.
   - Example: *The first speaker supports [opinion]. They believe this because [reason 1] and [reason 2].*
3. **Opposer’s Opinion:**
   - Briefly explain the reasons for disagreement.
   - Example: *The second speaker disagrees, arguing that [reason 1]. They also believe that [reason 2].*
4. **Conclusion:**
   - Summarize both sides.
   - Example: *In summary, the two speakers have different views on [topic], with one supporting it due to [reasons], and the other opposing it due to [reasons].*

## Common phrases**, **vocabulary**, and **collocations

### **1. Phrases for Introducing the Topic**

- **The announcement/notice proposes that...**
- **The speaker in the conversation expresses their opinion about...**
- **According to the announcement, the university plans to...**
- **The notice outlines a new policy regarding...**
- **The conversation discusses a proposal to...**

------

### **2. Phrases for Expressing Opinions**

- **The speaker agrees/disagrees with the proposal.**
- **The woman in the conversation believes that...**
- **The man thinks that this is/is not a good idea because...**
- **The speaker supports/opposes the plan due to two main reasons.**
- **She/he argues that...**
- **The speaker feels strongly that...**
- **In her/his opinion, this plan will/will not work because...**

------

### **3. Phrases for Providing Reasons**

- **The first reason is that...**
- **To begin with...**
- **For instance/For example...**
- **One major reason is...**
- **Additionally/Moreover...**
- **This is because...**
- **She/he highlights that...**
- **They mention that...**
- **Another point she/he raises is that...**
- **She/he supports this by stating that...**

------

### **4. Phrases for Drawing Conclusions**

- **In conclusion, the speaker is against/supports this proposal because...**
- **To sum up, the speaker has mixed feelings about the plan.**
- **Overall, she/he believes that the plan will/will not be effective due to...**
- **As a result, she/he thinks this plan is unnecessary/beneficial.**

- *Moreover*, *Additionally*, *Also*, *Furthermore*, *Not only that*, *In addition*.

#### **To Give Examples**

- *For instance*, *For example*, *Such as*, *As an illustration*, *To demonstrate*.

#### **To Contrast**

- *However*, *On the other hand*, *Nevertheless*, *Although*, *While it may be true that...*.

#### **To Conclude**

- *In conclusion*, *To summarize*, *Overall*, *As a result*, *Therefore*, *To sum up*.
