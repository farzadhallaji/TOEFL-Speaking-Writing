### Skill Enhancement

### **Q14: Some people prefer jobs that require them to work mostly with computers and other technology. Others prefer jobs that do not require them to work with much technology. Which type of job would you prefer? Why?**
1. "Technology-based jobs **enhance skills** in areas like data analysis and software proficiency, which are highly valued in today’s market."
2. "Non-technical roles often **improve communication** and interpersonal abilities, fostering creativity and collaboration."
3. "Choosing either role depends on whether you aim to **develop abilities** in tech-driven fields or focus on enhancing interpersonal skills."

### **Q26: Some university students participate in clubs or activities in addition to doing schoolwork. Other students spend most of their time doing schoolwork. Which do you think is better and why?**
1. "Clubs and activities **develop abilities** like leadership and teamwork, which are crucial for professional growth."
2. "Balancing academics with extracurriculars allows students to **enhance skills** in organization and time management."
3. "Engaging in both provides opportunities to **acquire knowledge** beyond the classroom, enriching the university experience."

2. "A strong online presence allows companies to **gain expertise** in customer behavior and preferences through analytics."
3. "Effective use of social platforms **enhances skills** in marketing, communication, and trend adaptation, essential for modern businesses."

### **Q104: Can students learn as effectively from online videos as from classroom teaching?**
1. "Online videos provide flexibility and opportunities to **gain expertise** in specialized topics not always covered in classrooms."
2. "Classroom teaching **improves communication** through real-time interaction and feedback from instructors."
3. "Both methods **enhance skills**, with videos offering convenience and classrooms fostering collaboration and discussion."

### **Q83: The local elementary school in your area has decided to make a change to their science program. Instead of children performing experiments themselves in science class, they will now watch demonstrations of science experiments on videos. Do you think this is a good idea? Why or why not?**

1. "Watching demonstrations can **enhance skills** in observation, as students focus on the steps and outcomes without distractions."
2. "However, performing experiments themselves allows children to **develop abilities** in problem-solving and hands-on application, which are crucial for understanding scientific principles."
3. "A balanced approach, combining video demonstrations with practical experiments, ensures students **acquire knowledge** effectively while building confidence in their skills."
