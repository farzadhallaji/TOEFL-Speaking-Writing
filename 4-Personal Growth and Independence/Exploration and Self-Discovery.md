### Exploration and Self-Discovery


### **Q24: Do first impressions tell you a lot about a person, or does it take time to truly know someone? Why?**
1. "First impressions can inspire initial curiosity, but deeper **self-discovery** happens over time as relationships grow."
2. "Getting to know someone fully requires time to **uncover potential** traits and layers of their personality beyond the surface."
3. "While first impressions offer glimpses, meaningful connections depend on the effort to **broaden horizons** through ongoing interaction."

---

### **Q25: Should people spend a lot of money on vacations or choose budget-friendly travel? Why?**
1. "Budget-friendly travel encourages **exploration and self-discovery**, as it often involves authentic and off-the-beaten-path experiences."
2. "Splurging on unique destinations can **enrich life experiences**, offering luxurious comfort while exposing travelers to new cultures."
3. "Both options allow travelers to **broaden horizons**, depending on their goals for relaxation or adventure."

---

### **Q36: Should recent graduates volunteer before starting their careers? Why?**
1. "Volunteering offers graduates a chance to **explore passions** and discover fields they might not have considered before."
2. "These experiences foster **personal growth**, teaching adaptability and compassion through real-world challenges."
3. "By engaging in volunteer work, young professionals can **uncover potential** career paths and personal strengths."

---

### **Q57: Some universities expect students to choose a major field of study when they enter university. Other universities allow students to wait until the second or third year before deciding on a major field of study. Which do you think is better? Why?**
1. "Delaying the choice of a major allows students to **explore passions**, ensuring their decisions align with their interests and talents."
2. "This approach encourages **self-discovery**, as students have the flexibility to experiment with diverse fields before committing."
3. "Waiting to declare a major can **broaden horizons**, as students gain exposure to multiple disciplines and perspectives."
