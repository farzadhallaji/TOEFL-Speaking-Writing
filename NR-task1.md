## **1. General Overview of TOEFL Speaking Tasks**

The TOEFL Speaking section consists of four tasks:

1. **Independent Task:** You express your personal opinion or preference.
2. **Integrated Reading + Listening Task:** You summarize a passage and lecture.
3. **Integrated Listening Task:** You summarize a conversation or lecture.
4. **Integrated Lecture Task:** You explain information from a lecture.

This guide focuses on providing reusable phrases, structures, and transitions to craft high-quality responses across all tasks.

------

## **2. Universal Introduction Phrases**

### **For Independent Tasks**

- "I personally prefer [choice A] over [choice B] because..."
- "In my opinion, [choice A] is a better option due to [reason 1] and [reason 2]."
- "For me, [choice A] is more appealing for two main reasons."
- "I believe that [choice A] offers more advantages because..."
- "If I had to choose between [choice A] and [choice B], I would definitely choose [choice A]. Here’s why."

### **For Integrated Tasks**

- "The reading and lecture discuss [topic]."
- "The passage explains [main idea], while the lecture provides additional information/challenges the idea by stating that..."
- "According to the passage, [key point]. However, the lecturer disagrees by explaining that..."
- "The conversation between the students focuses on [topic], where one student suggests that..."
- "In the lecture, the professor explains [topic] by highlighting [key points]."

## **3. Phrases for the Body of the Response**

- "Firstly,..."
- "The first reason is that..."
- "My primary reason for this is..."

- "To begin with,..."

### **Explaining with Examples**

- "For example,..."
- "A good illustration of this is..."
- "One instance of this is..."
- "From my personal experience,..."
- "To illustrate this point,..."

### **Adding More Reasons**

- "Secondly,..."
- "Another reason is that..."
- "Additionally,..."
- "What’s more,..."
- "Furthermore,..."

### **Contrasting Ideas**

- "On the other hand,..."
- "In contrast,..."
- "However,..."
- "Although...,"
- "While some may argue that..., I believe..."

### **Explaining Outcomes**

- "This leads to..."
- "As a result,..."
- "Therefore,..."
- "Consequently,..."
- "Because of this,..."

## **7. Sample Templates for Common Question Types**

### **Preference Questions**

#### Template:

1. Introduction:
   - "I prefer [choice A] over [choice B] for two main reasons."
2. Reason 1 + Example:
   - "Firstly, [reason 1]. For example, [specific instance]. This shows that [explanation]."
3. Reason 2 + Example:
   - "Secondly, [reason 2]. For instance, [specific example]. This proves that [explanation]."
4. Conclusion:
   - "Overall, [choice A] is better because of [reason 1] and [reason 2]."

------

### **Agree/Disagree Questions**

#### Template:

1. Introduction:
   - "I agree/disagree with the statement that [restate statement] for two reasons."
2. Reason 1 + Example:
   - "The first reason is that [reason 1]. For example, [specific example]. This demonstrates that [explanation]."
3. Reason 2 + Example:
   - "The second reason is that [reason 2]. For instance, [specific example]. This highlights that [explanation]."
4. Conclusion:
   - "In conclusion, I believe that [restate opinion] because of [reason 1] and [reason 2]."
