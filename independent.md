## Templates

### **Template 1: Agree/Disagree with a Statement**

1. **Introduction**:

   - State your position clearly.
   - Provide a brief overview of your supporting points.

   *Example*: "I firmly agree/disagree with the idea that [statement]. In my opinion, [reason 1] and [reason 2] make this perspective more valid."

2. **Body Paragraph 1**:

   - Present your first supporting reason.
   - Use examples or experiences to illustrate your point.

   *Example*: "Firstly, [reason 1]. For instance, [specific example or personal anecdote]. This demonstrates that [conclusion related to reason 1]."

3. **Body Paragraph 2**:

   - Present your second supporting reason.
   - Provide additional examples or evidence.

   *Example*: "Secondly, [reason 2]. A relevant example is [another example or situation]. This underscores that [conclusion related to reason 2]."

4. **Conclusion**:

   - Summarize your main points.
   - Reaffirm your stance.

   *Example*: "In conclusion, [restate main argument and reasons]. Therefore, I strongly believe that [restated position]."

------

### **Template 2: Compare Two Perspectives**

1. **Introduction**:

   - Introduce the two perspectives.
   - State which one you agree with and why.

   *Example*: "While some people believe [perspective 1], others argue that [perspective 2]. I am inclined to agree with the latter/former because [reason 1] and [reason 2]."

2. **Body Paragraph 1**:

   - Discuss the strengths of your chosen perspective.
   - Provide examples or scenarios to support it.

   *Example*: "One of the main reasons I support [chosen perspective] is [reason 1]. For instance, [specific example]. This shows that [explanation]."

3. **Body Paragraph 2**:

   - Acknowledge the opposing perspective.
   - Explain why it is less compelling or flawed.

   *Example*: "Although [opposing perspective] has its merits, it fails to address [weakness or limitation]. For example, [counterexample]."

4. **Conclusion**:

   - Reiterate your preference and summarize your reasoning.

   *Example*: "In summary, while [opposing perspective] offers some valid points, I believe [chosen perspective] is more beneficial because [reasons]."

------

### **Template 3: Discuss the Advantages/Disadvantages of a Proposal**

1. **Introduction**:

   - Introduce the proposal or topic.
   - State whether you believe the advantages outweigh the disadvantages or vice versa.

   *Example*: "The idea of [proposal] has both advantages and disadvantages. However, I believe the benefits/drawbacks outweigh the drawbacks/benefits due to [reason 1] and [reason 2]."

2. **Body Paragraph 1**:

   - Highlight the main advantages or disadvantages.
   - Use examples to illustrate your point.

   *Example*: "One significant advantage/disadvantage of [proposal] is [reason 1]. For example, [specific case or evidence]."

3. **Body Paragraph 2**:

   - Discuss the secondary advantage or disadvantage.
   - Provide additional support.

   *Example*: "Another important advantage/disadvantage is [reason 2]. For instance, [another example]."

4. **Conclusion**:

   - Summarize the advantages or disadvantages and your position.

   *Example*: "In conclusion, while [proposal] has its challenges/merits, the overall [advantages/disadvantages] make it a [positive/negative] idea."

------

### **Template 4: Propose a Solution or Recommendation**

1. **Introduction**:

   - State the problem or issue.
   - Introduce your proposed solution or recommendation.

   *Example*: "One of the most pressing challenges today is [problem]. I believe that [solution] is the most effective way to address this issue because [reason 1] and [reason 2]."

2. **Body Paragraph 1**:

   - Explain your first reason for supporting the solution.
   - Provide an example or evidence.

   *Example*: "Firstly, [reason 1]. For example, [specific example]. This shows that [explanation]."

3. **Body Paragraph 2**:

   - Elaborate on the second reason.
   - Include additional examples or evidence.

   *Example*: "Secondly, [reason 2]. For instance, [another case]. This demonstrates the practicality of [solution]."

4. **Conclusion**:

   - Restate the problem and summarize your solution.
   - Emphasize its importance.

   *Example*: "In summary, [solution] effectively addresses [problem] by [reasons]. Implementing this solution is crucial for [desired outcome]."
