 

## Templates

#### **Template 1: Explaining a Concept with Examples**

1. Introduction
   - "The reading passage introduces the concept of [concept name], which refers to [brief explanation of the concept]."
2. Link to the Lecture
   - "In the lecture, the professor elaborates on this concept by providing an example of [briefly describe the example]."
3. Details from the Example
   - "The professor explains that [detailed description of how the example supports the concept]."
4. Conclusion
   - "This example clearly illustrates [concept name] because [summarize why the example is relevant]."

------

#### **Template 2: Explaining Scientific Phenomena**

1. Introduction
   - "The reading discusses [phenomenon] and describes it as [brief explanation]."
2. Transition to the Lecture
   - "The professor supports this explanation by discussing an example involving [describe the scenario in the lecture]."
3. Lecture Details
   - "The professor explains that [specific details about the example]."
   - "Furthermore, [additional points or outcomes mentioned in the lecture]."
4. Summary
   - "This example effectively demonstrates the [phenomenon] by showing how [summarize the connection between the concept and the example]."

------

#### **Template 3: Demonstrating Behavioral Theories**

1. Opening
   - "The reading describes [theory/concept] as [brief explanation]."
2. Support from the Lecture
   - "The professor provides an example of [describe the specific case] to illustrate this."
3. Detailed Explanation
   - "In the example, [explain the process or scenario discussed in the lecture]."
   - "This behavior aligns with the theory because [tie it back to the theory's principles]."
4. Conclusion
   - "Thus, the professor’s example effectively illustrates [theory/concept] by [summary of the connection]."

------

#### **Template 4: Comparing Actions or Behaviors**

1. Introduction
   - "According to the reading, [concept] involves [definition or principle]."
2. Lecture Example
   - "To clarify this, the professor uses the example of [describe the example]."
3. Details
   - "The professor explains that [provide details of the example]."
   - "This shows how [tie the specific actions or behaviors in the example to the concept]."
4. Wrap-Up
   - "This comparison demonstrates [concept] effectively by showing [summarize the insight]."

------

#### **Template 5: Illustrating Marketing or Business Concepts**

1. Introduction
   - "The reading passage defines [marketing/business concept] as [brief explanation]."
2. Illustration in the Lecture
   - "The professor builds on this idea by describing an example of [describe example]."
3. Application
   - "In the example, [explain how the professor's case ties into the marketing/business concept]."
   - "This demonstrates how [specific element] is achieved through [concept]."
4. Conclusion
   - "The professor’s example successfully highlights [concept] by showing [conclusion]."

## Common phrases, collocations, and vocabulary 

**1. General Academic Phrases**

- Introducing concepts:
  - "The reading passage introduces the concept of..."
  - "According to the reading, [concept] refers to..."
  - "The professor elaborates on this by providing an example of..."
  - "The lecture builds on the idea by explaining..."
- Explaining examples:
  - "The professor uses the example of [specific example] to clarify..."
  - "In the lecture, [example] is described as..."
  - "This example demonstrates the concept because..."
  - "By doing this, [subject of example] illustrates the main idea of..."
- Summarizing:
  - "In summary, the professor’s example shows how..."
  - "This effectively illustrates the concept by..."
  - "Overall, the reading and lecture work together to show that..."
